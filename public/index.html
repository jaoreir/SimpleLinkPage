Linux

Since May 2024 I've been daily driving Linux on all of my computers.

At first, I was on NixOS to benefit from its declarative configuration system. From doing so I learned a lot about how Linux works and some concepts from functional programming. I also had a lot of fun customizing a hyprland desktop environment.

But ultimately I was too bothered by the instability of a rolling release distribution, the incompatibilities of a few programs I wanted to run, and the effort required to just make things work.

So I decided to switch to Kubuntu in June 2025 and sticked to almost all default settings. I felt like this was a way more time-efficient approach to using Linux. Currently I'm pretty happy about it.


Modded Minecraft

The video game I spend the most time playing is modded Minecraft. I currently have a singleplayer forever world started back in Oct 2024. My modlist is always changing, but the selection is always focused on compatibility, building, farming, and creating history inside the world.

The main mods I use are:

Farmer's Delight, the Let's Do series, and other farming mods

These mods provide a lot of new crops and meals to cook. A lot of the meals are also placeable in the world, which is great for interiors.

Via Romana

A mod that allows you to fast travel after creating paths between locations. It incentivises the building of transportation networks, which is in line with what I like to do.

Exposure

This mod adds cameras and photos to the game, which makes recording the history of the world a lot more immersive and fun. I love taking pictures of random weird stuff I come across and pictures of newly constructed builds.

Touhou Little Maid

This mod provides lively NPCs based on characters from the Touhou Project that walk around and do work, making builds feel more alive. The maid stuff feels weird though. I don't want a companion in game.

Iron's Spells and Spellbooks

An easy magic mod for a casual player like me. The teleport spell is especially useful for building in survival. Another favourite spell of mine is the summon fireworks spell. It's really fun to explode everything in spectacular colour.

Create

Create is used to generate resources for building, and to provide logistics networks for better inventory management. While other tech mods can do the same, create machines usually look better, so that's why it's my pick.

RUMBLE VR

I also play a 1v1 VR fighting game called RUMBLE where you summon rocks to throw at each other. It has one of the most positive gaming communities I've ever seen. It's also a fun way to exercise :D

However, I seem to be one of the only Asian players around. So I usually have to play cross region and suffer from very high latency. This pulled me away from getting good at the game, but pushed me to try out more unconventional playstyles to find my own fun.
